Stamp Regulations in Malaysia

Understanding the legal framework for stamps and printing services in Malaysia.

Stamp Act 1949 (Revised 1989)

The Stamp Act 1949 is the primary legislation governing stamp duties in Malaysia. It outlines the duties payable on various instruments and documents, including commercial agreements, property transfers, and legal documents. The Act is administered by the Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia (Lembaga Hasil Dalam Negeri, LHDN).

Company Stamps & Common Seals

Under the Companies Act 2016 (Act 777), Malaysian companies are no longer required to have a common seal. However, many businesses continue to use company stamps and common seals as a practical means of authentication. When a company chooses to use a common seal, it must comply with the regulations set out in the company's constitution.

Company rubber stamps (cop syarikat) are widely used for everyday business operations such as endorsing documents, receipts, invoices, and official correspondence. While not legally mandated, they serve as an important tool for professional identification.

Business Registration Requirements

Businesses involved in the manufacture and supply of stamps and printing services in Malaysia must be registered with:

  • Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia (SSM) — Companies Commission of Malaysia for business registration
  • Kementerian Kewangan Malaysia (MOF) — Ministry of Finance for government procurement eligibility
  • Lembaga Hasil Dalam Negeri (LHDN) — Inland Revenue Board for tax compliance

MOF Registration Benefits

Being registered with the Ministry of Finance (Kementerian Kewangan) allows a business to participate in government procurement and supply tenders. This registration certifies that the company meets the standards and requirements set by the Malaysian government for the supply of goods and services. PolyStamp & Print Services holds an active MOF registration, ensuring our business operations meet national standards.

Consumer Protection

All stamp and printing services in Malaysia are subject to the Consumer Protection Act 1999, which safeguards buyers' rights including the right to quality products, accurate descriptions, and fair business practices. As a registered business, PolyStamp adheres to all consumer protection regulations to ensure customer satisfaction and trust.
